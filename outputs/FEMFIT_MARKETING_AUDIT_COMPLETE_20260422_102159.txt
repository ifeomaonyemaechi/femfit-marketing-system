**Revenue Impact:** Email currently drives ~R33,000-66,000/month (22% of R150-300K). SMS at 20% of email = R6,600-13,200/month potential.

## Competitive Analysis: FemFit.fit vs Gymshark

### COMPETITIVE POSITION VS GYMSHARK:
FemFit.fit is operating at roughly 0.1% of Gymshark's scale, making direct competition unrealistic. However, this isn't the relevant comparison—Gymshark serves as a category blueprint, not a competitor for the same customers. FemFit.fit's 1.9% engagement rate and 18.2% email open rate suggest a lukewarm audience connection, and with only 2 of 5 email flows active and no paid acquisition, the brand is significantly under-investing in the growth levers Gymshark mastered early.

### WHAT FEMFIT.FIT CAN LEARN:

1. **Adopt the 70/30 content ratio.** Gymshark built loyalty by making most content about the customer's journey, not the product. FemFit.fit should audit current content—if it's product-heavy, shift toward transformation stories, workout tips, and community spotlights.

2. **Build behavioural email triggers.** Gymshark's segmentation drives relevance. FemFit.fit should activate those dormant 3 flows immediately—abandoned cart, post-purchase, and win-back sequences are non-negotiable at this stage.

### WHERE FEMFIT.FIT CAN WIN:

1. **Radical personalisation.** At 8,400 followers, FemFit.fit can reply to every comment, DM new followers personally, and feature real customers by name. This builds loyalty Gymshark physically cannot replicate.

2. **Niche authority positioning.** Gymshark serves everyone; FemFit.fit can own a specific segment (e.g., women returning to fitness postpartum, beginners intimidated by gym culture). Specificity beats scale at this stage.

### THREAT LEVEL: Low
Gymshark isn't competing for FemFit.fit's customers—the real threat is FemFit.fit failing to learn from Gymshark's playbook while smaller, hungrier competitors do.
