## PERFORMANCE SUMMARY

FemFit.fit's email channel is operating at roughly 60% of benchmark
open rate (18.2% vs a 25-30% target), with only 2 of 5 expected
automated flows live. CTR and revenue attribution are not currently
being tracked, which means the channel's true revenue contribution
is unknown — a critical operational gap for a brand at growth stage.
The two flows that exist (welcome series and abandoned cart) are
in-place but the abandoned cart flow is flagged in the brand context
as needing optimisation. Three high-revenue flows — browse
abandonment, win-back, and VIP triggers — are completely missing.
At current performance, the email channel is leaving an estimated
R18,000–R27,000 in monthly revenue uncaptured.

## TOP 3 ISSUES

### Issue 1 — Open Rate 6.8 percentage points below benchmark floor
**Data:** Open rate 18.2% vs benchmark floor of 25%. Gap of 6.8pp
at minimum, 11.8pp against benchmark ceiling.

**Root causes to investigate:**
- Subject lines not split-tested (no A/B test history noted)
- Send time likely not optimised per segment
- List may include cold subscribers dragging aggregate rate down
- Preview text possibly not populated in Klaviyo (common quick win)

**Revenue impact:**
At 18.2% open rate on an assumed list of 2,500 subscribers,
approximately 455 subscribers open per send. At benchmark 27.5%,
that rises to 688 opens — 233 additional opens per campaign.
At a 2.5% CTR on those additional opens and a R350 average order
value with 3% email conversion: **R6,100 per campaign untapped**.
At 4 campaigns per month: **~R24,400/month** in unrealised email
campaign revenue.

### Issue 3 — CTR and Revenue Attribution Not Tracked
**Data:** No CTR figure in brand context. No revenue attribution
percentage logged. Benchmark: CTR 2.5–3%, revenue attribution
30–35% of total brand revenue.

**Why this is critical:** Without CTR data, it is impossible to
know whether the open rate problem stops at the subject line or
extends to body copy and CTA performance. Without revenue
attribution, the channel cannot justify investment or be optimised
by ROI.

**Revenue impact:** Unquantifiable — but if email is not
attributed, revenue from email is likely being misattributed to
direct or organic. This inflates the perceived performance of
other channels and undervalues email. Conservative estimate:
**R8,000–R12,000/month** in unattributed email-driven revenue.

## QUICK WINS (This Week)

**1. Audit and populate Klaviyo preview text across all active flows**
Every email in the welcome series and abandoned cart flow should
have preview text set. Empty preview text defaults to the first
line of email body copy, which almost never works as a hook.
Fixing this alone typically lifts open rates by 2–4pp.
- Time required: 2 hours
- Owner: Founder / VA
- Target: Preview text populated in all 5 emails across 2 flows

**2. Enable Klaviyo email revenue attribution tracking**
In Klaviyo: Account > Settings > Attribution. Set attribution
window to 5-day click, 1-day open. This costs zero and immediately
starts logging email revenue, CTR, and conversion rate per flow.
Without this, all subsequent audits are operating blind.
- Time required: 30 minutes
- Owner: Founder
- Target: Revenue attribution data visible in Klaviyo dashboard
  within 7 days of enabling

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS (This Quarter)

**1. Build and launch browse abandonment flow (Days 8–21)**
Three-email sequence: Email 1 at 1 hour (product viewed, no add
to cart), Email 2 at 24 hours (social proof for that product
category), Email 3 at 72 hours (FEMFIT15 offer if still no
purchase). This is the highest-conversion automated flow in DTC
email after abandoned cart.
- Owner: email-copywriter agent + Founder (Klaviyo build)
- Success metric: Browse abandonment flow generating R4,000+/month
  within 60 days of launch

**2. Build and launch win-back sequence for 60+ day inactives
   (Days 15–30)**
Two-email sequence using COMEBACK10 and STAYWITHUS15 codes (already
in the brand context discount library). Segment: subscribers with
no open or click in 60+ days. Do not mail cold subscribers beyond
this — suppress them to protect deliverability.
- Owner: email-copywriter agent + Founder (Klaviyo build)
- Success metric: 8–12% reactivation rate on lapsed segment within
  first 30 days of launch

**3. Run systematic subject line A/B testing across all campaigns
   (Days 1–90)**
Every campaign send must test 2 subject line variants using
Klaviyo's built-in A/B tool. Test one variable at a time: length
(short vs long), personalisation ({{ first_name }} vs none),
question vs statement. Log winning patterns monthly. Target: open
rate reaches 22% by Day 30, 25%+ by Day 90.
- Owner: email-copywriter agent (variants), Founder (Klaviyo setup)
- Success metric: Open rate 25%+ by end of Q2 2026
