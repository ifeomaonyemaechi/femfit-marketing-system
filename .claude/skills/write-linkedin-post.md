# Skill: Write LinkedIn Post
# FemFit.fit Marketing Operations System

## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES
Generates LinkedIn posts for FemFit.fit. Called by the
social-media-manager agent. LinkedIn content for FemFit.fit
serves a dual purpose — building brand awareness with
potential stockists, wholesale partners, and press contacts,
while also reaching women who train and follow fitness brands
on LinkedIn.

## WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
Use when:
- Weekly content pack includes a LinkedIn piece
- A brand milestone needs announcing
- A behind-the-scenes founder story is planned
- A product launch needs B2B visibility

## LINKEDIN AUDIENCE FOR FEMFIT.FIT
Primary: Women 28-45 who train and follow fitness brands
Secondary: Retail buyers, wholesale partners, press contacts
Tertiary: Other founders and entrepreneurs who train

## LINKEDIN VS INSTAGRAM — KEY DIFFERENCES
LinkedIn posts can be longer and more reflective.
The platform rewards insight, not just aesthetics.
Stories about building the brand perform well here.
Educational content with a business angle resonates.
No hashtag stuffing — 3-5 relevant hashtags maximum.

## POST FORMATS

### Founder Story
What it is: A personal story about building FemFit.fit
or a lesson learned through training or business.
Length: 150-250 words
Structure: Hook → Story → Lesson → CTA
Best for: Brand building, humanising the founder

### Product Truth
What it is: An honest, specific take on what makes
a FemFit.fit product different. Written as insight,
not as an ad.
Length: 100-150 words
Structure: Observation → Proof → CTA
Best for: Reaching buyers and press contacts

### Training Philosophy
What it is: A short, direct post about why women
train and what gear should do for them.
Length: 100-150 words
Structure: Bold statement → Expansion → Tie to brand
Best for: Reaching target customer on LinkedIn

### Milestone or Behind the Scenes
What it is: A transparent update about building
the brand — a launch, a decision made, a lesson
from a failure.
Length: 150-200 words
Structure: What happened → What it meant → What is next
Best for: Press, partners, and community building

## LINKEDIN FORMATTING RULES
- First line is the hook — must work as a standalone
  statement that stops the scroll
- Use single line breaks between paragraphs — LinkedIn
  collapses text so white space matters
- No bullet points in the opening — save for body
- End with one question or one clear CTA
- Hashtags go at the very end — 3-5 maximum
- Always use: #femfit #womensfitness plus 1-3 niche tags

## TONE ON LINKEDIN
More reflective than Instagram. Less raw than TikTok.
Still direct — no corporate language.
Founder voice works best — first person, specific,
honest about the process of building something real.
